Contest Overview

The goal of Puzzle Point is to build confidence in females by providing them a network of other girls interested in STEM for mutual support and by using puzzles to teach logical reasoning skills central to the study of mathematics. Researchers have found that consistent puzzle play improves spatial recognition, mental rotation of objects, and 3-D visualization. Thus puzzles can be valuable tools to teach girls quantitative reasoning skills that later translate into confidence and concept mastery during math classes. Furthermore, the emphasis on creative thinking and stress-free competition is more likely to encourage participation and foster inclusion.

Gender Disparity in STEM

Puzzle Point is the first ever puzzle day adventure for female high school students in New York City. Our mission is to foster excitement for mathematics through enjoyable puzzles, which utilize the same skill set of logical thinking and quantitative reasoning. Led by a team of women in STEM, we hope to create a supportive atmosphere for female students to collaborate and build confidence through conquering problems together.
